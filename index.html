About Me

I started out as a physics major at the University of Arizona in 2011. I took a computational physics class, and fell in love with programming. Since then, I have switched my major to computer science, added a math major, and kept physics and Spanish around as minors.

Once I knew that computer science and software development was for me, I dove head-first into the community. I have been involved in the ACM Student Chapter, taking on the roles of vice president and president over the last two years. Furthermore I am an active member of the Tucson Users of Functional Programming, and have organized and volunteered at events such as the UofA ACM Hackathon, Open Source Day, and Programming Day for Kids.
